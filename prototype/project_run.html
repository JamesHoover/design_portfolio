Problem Statement

Young adult voters (age 18-29) need a way to vote efficiently because the current voting system is too complex for them to invest their time.

Research Insights

  • Users feel the current voting process is too complicated and time-consuming for the amount of impact that their vote carries.
  • Users do not feel well informed on current issues and candidates. They also have trouble finding a candidate that aligns well with their own views.
  • Users do not know about or keep track of important dates leading up to and regarding elections.

High Level Objectives

  • Allow the user to vote for the candidate of their choice.
  • Allow for the user to become well informed on the current issues and candidates.
  • Allow the user to view important dates regarding elections.

Personas

Sam is a 21 year old stylist. She has voted once in a presidential election. She does not like to follow politics because it is overwhelming and stresses her out.

Tim is an 18 year old student. He has never voted and has little idea what to expect. The only issue on the ballot he is aware of is voting for the president. He gets most of his information from his parents and social media. He wants to do his civil duty but does not feel like hes making his own choice, just the choice of his parents and popular opinion.

Fred is a 25 year old developer. He is active on social media. He follows elections superficially but does not care to vote because the time commitment does not outweigh his vote's impact.

Jay is a 29 year old sales rep. He stays relatively informed. He will not vote just to vote. He only dedicates the time to vote when its something he truly believes in.
