Problem Statement

US citizens need a way to protect themselves and their personal information because they are worried that their information is being viewed, stored, and/or explointed by the government and corporations.

Research Insights

  • Users feel that their privacy is being violated and they have no control to stop it.
  • Users do not want to give up any freedoms to the government for fear that the government will abuse their power and take more freedom.
  • "Those who would give up essential Liberty, to purchase a little temporary Safety, deserve neither Liberty nor Safety." -- Benjamin Franklin

High Level Objectives

  • Allow the user to protect themselves and their personal information.
  • Relinquish users fear of their privacy being corrupted.
  • Allow the user to see what data can and has been gathered by the government and how it is being used.

Personas

Tim is an 18 year old student. He has nothing to hide and sees a need for spying as necessary for proctection but still feels as though hes being watched whenever he interacts online. This is unsettling to him.

Sam is a 21 year old stylist. Why should the NSA be able to spy on us, especially if we are just normal, non harmful citizens. People have privacy, and that shouldn't be violated. I understand that there needs to be a system at which the government can protect America from potential threats such as terrorists, but isn't there another way?

Fred is a 25 year old developer. He actively hides his information is vocal about the governments abuse of power and the dangers he feels that it entails. Thinks that the spying doesnt even prevent terrorist attacks so its pointless.

Jay is a 29 year old sales rep. He is scared that we are following the path of Big Brother and that we should not give up any ground.
